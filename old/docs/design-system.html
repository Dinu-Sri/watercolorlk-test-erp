UX Rules

These are the rules I would carry into future development

This turns your brand and conversion requirements into repeatable UI constraints so future features stay consistent instead of drifting page by page.

Readable first

17px body text is the default. No thin weights for product descriptions, forms, filters, or legal text.

Trust before urgency

Navy structure leads. Red appears only for scarcity, low stock, and real urgency cues.

Short click depth

Homepage to category to product should stay within 2 to 3 clicks. Full purchase path should stay lean.

Whole card is clickable

Product cards act like single tap targets. Users should not hunt for tiny text links to continue shopping.

Proof near price

Ratings, sold count, trust cues, and delivery notes sit close to the price and CTA, not buried in tabs.

Guest-first checkout

Account creation should not block orders. Offer it after the transaction, not before it.
